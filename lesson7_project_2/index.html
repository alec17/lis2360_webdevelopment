How do i Build a PC?

Nowadays, everyone uses computers throughout most of their typical day. Whether it be using a phone, traditional PC, or even a smart television, computers have become a crucial part of everyone's lives. However, most people can't even name most of the parts that make up a computer. Without specific hardware, none of the computers we use would do anything.

Every computer consists of seven major parts. These parts include a: motherboard, processor (CPU), hard drive, power supply, graphics card, PCI-Express card, and memory (RAM). Each of these components do something specific and work together with each other in order to complete the task at hand. Some of these parts may seem familiar but most people are unaware pf what each part does.

Let's start with the motherboard. A motherboard's job is to hold all of the pieces needed in a computer and connect them so they can work together. Next, we have the processor, or Central Programming Unit (CPU). A CPU's job is to be the "brains" of the system and process everything that's happening. Thirdly, the hard drive is where the files and information on a computer are stored. Unlike a hard drive, which handles long-term memory, Random Access Memory (RAM) handles all of the short-term memory not needed for long-term use. Another crucial part is the Power Supply (PSU). This supplies electricity to the computer and allows it to "turn on." PCI-Express Cards add ports to the computer that allow it to connect to other computers so they can work toegther. Lastly, the Graphics Card of a computer is much like a CPU but, it only handles graphics specifically.
